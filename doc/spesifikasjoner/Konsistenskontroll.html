The numbering is based on the following succession of meteorological elements:

Air pressure (21-30), temperature/humidity (31-40), wind/waves (41-60), precipitation/snow/ state of the ground (61-80), visibility/weather codes (81-120) and cloud codes (121-180). The KVALOBS naming convention (1) (based on DNMIs naming convention (2)) and synop-/ship-codes are used in the table below, on the left and right, respectively. The algorithms concerning SYNOP reports are taken from WMO-No.305 (3) (WMO) or from earlier practise at met.no (DNMI). SHIP reports are taken from the Turbo programme (6) (WMO). Also a few Nordic algorithms are used. The asterisk means that the WMO algorithm is in use at met.no.


Comment to the nomenclature below

Min(parameter_12h) means minimum of all the values of the parameter during the last 12 hours. For synop and ship messages this means that the 12 hours old parameter value and the actual value are both included.


Max(parameter_12h) has a corresponding meaning.


Null, “x” and “/” means missing value.

QC1-6: Konsistens-kontroll – klimatologisk samsvar mellom parametere


The numbering is based on the following succession of meteorological elements:

Air pressure (201-210), temperature (211-230), temperature/weather (231-270), wind/weather/waves/state of the sea (271-300), precipitation/snow/state of the ground (301-310), visibility/weather codes (311-320) and cloud/weather codes (321-330). The same naming convention is used as in the preceding chapter.


It is strongly recommended that KVALOBS choose the most efficient tests among the broad range of tests below and adjusts the test values and that the quality control system have the possibility of turning off/on unfortunate tests. It must be easy to change test values when it is appropriate.

Some comments to the method


  • Daily observations of precipitation, snow depth, snow cover and weather symbols are required.


  • Empirically values of testing limits are selected.


  • There is no automatic flagging. The corrections are manually flagged during the HQC-process.


  • The recommended quality control level is QC2 of daily values.


  • The frequency of control to day is weekly or monthly, but it should be performed as soon as daily values are received, i.e. every day.


  • Drawbacks of the method are that changes on snow depth due to snowdrift are not taken care of. Snowdrift should be included in the weather symbols at precipitation stations. This would make it possible to take wind effects during the winter into account in the algorithm.


  • For weather stations also the temperature could be used to refine the algorithm.


  • Advantages of the method is that it is an efficient algorithm to track several kind of errors, especially errors caused by mismatch between the parameters; i.e. reported weather symbols and reported precipitation / snow depth. The main cause of these errors is the manual transmittance (observations denoted on cards sent once a week by post) of the observations once a week. The manual correction of traced errors makes it possible to correct the data in the probably best way.


  • Concerning practical experience and performance it can be stated that this is an efficient and practical tool. The error log tracks only real errors.



Further documentation can be found in Report no. 01/95 KLIBAS “Geofysisk konsistenskontroll av nedbørdata i arbeidslager”.

Skisse til program

Programmet retter automatisk feil i værsymboler og værkoder på mest sannsynlig måte og uten at det får store konsekvenser om det rettes feil. Observatørens bevisste valg av værsymboler og værkoder kan begge være retningsgivende for selve rettelsen. F.eks.: Dersom værkodene angir nedbør, føyes manglende værsymboler til. Dersom værsymboler angir nedbør, rettes uriktige værkoder i overens­stemmelse med dette. NB! Men dersom programmet ikke finner noen god måte å rette inkonsistens på, avhengig av vær­situasjonen og av hvilken stasjon vi har å gjøre med, etc, bør det fortsatt rettes manuelt (senere i prosessen), inntil bedre metoder er utviklet og satt i system.


Et korreksjonsprogram som skal takle alt må nødvendigvis inneholde en rekke usannsynlige kombinasjoner, som antagelig ytterst sjelden vil forekomme. Noen slike kombinasjoner er det sikkert ikke tenkt på, men når de forekommer bør de noteres og tas med i neste versjon. Forhåpentligvis har vi fått med de fleste av de gale kombinasjonene, som stadig går igjen.

Nåværende kontroll av PO, AA og PP ved aktuelt og forrige observasjonstidspunkt.


1. | (PO(t-6) + PP(t-6)) – (PO(t) – PP(t)) | < 2 hPa ikke utskrift avslutt

Algoritmen skal leses på følgende måte: Dersom betingelse 1 slår til, avsluttes testen uten utskrift. Dersom betingelse 1 ikke slår til, testes betingelse 2. Dersom betingelse 3 slår til, skrives trykk-karakteristikken, AA, ut og testen avsluttes. Dersom betingelse 3 ikke slår til, testes betingelse 4. Dersom betingelse 4 slår til, skrives barometer­tendensen, PP, ut.
