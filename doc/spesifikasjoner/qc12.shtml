QC1-2: Certain consistency - formal relationship between parameters


The numbering is based on the following succession of meteorological elements:

Air pressure (21-30), temperature/humidity (31-40), wind/waves (41-60), precipitation/snow/ state of the ground (61-80), visibility/weather codes (81-120) and cloud codes (121-180). The KVALOBS naming convention (1) and the WMO synop-/ship-codes are used in the table below on the left and right, respectively. The algorithms concerning SYNOP reports are taken from WMO-No.305 (3) (WMO) or from earlier practise at met.no (DNMI). The algorithms concerning SHIP reports are taken from the Turbowin programme (6) (WMO). Also a few Nordic algorithms are used. The asterisk means that the WMO algorithm has been in maunal use previously at met.no.


Comment to the nomenclature below

Min(parameter_12h) means minimum of all the values of the parameter during the last 12 hours. For synop and ship messages this means that the 12 hours old parameter value and the actual value are both included.


Max(parameter_12h) has a corresponding meaning.


Null, “x” and “/” means missing value.

QC1-2-72.b Internal consistency in precipitation observations

The control is applied when the complete observation from the previous 24 hours is available as

  • amount of precipitation (RR_24)
  • national weather symbol (precipitation manner) V1-V7
  • snow depth (SA)
Also the charcteristic diurnal precipitation by month of the year is used (table 2 below).

The daily characteristic Dkar is computed from the weather symbols and the change in snow depth [SA - SA(-24h)] in cm. Combinations of precipitation weather codes is grouped between the characteristics “Rain”, “Snow” or “Dew/Hoar frost”.
Tests comparing Dkar with precipitation amount, snow depth, snow cover and change in snow depth, are performed.
Suspicious observations are flagged.
